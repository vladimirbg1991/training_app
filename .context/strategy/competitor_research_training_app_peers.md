# Competitor Pain-Point Audit: What Reddit Hates About Strength & Fitness Tracking Apps (and How a New Lifting App Can Win)

**TL;DR**
- The single biggest landmine in this category is **converting previously-free features into a subscription** — Strong, StrongLifts 5x5, and MyFitnessPal each detonated their reputations doing it, and the bitterness on Reddit, the App Store and Google Play is years-deep. Don't sell "lifetime" loosely, don't paywall features users already had for free, and price your subscription as if you genuinely have to earn it every month.
- The second-biggest cluster of complaints is **friction during the actual workout**: too many taps to log a set, broken rest timers, Apple Watch / phone sync that drops mid-set, and data loss after updates (Gymshark Training is a textbook example). Lifters care more about a 2-tap log plus a rest timer that never fails than about analytics, social feeds, or AI.
- The third lesson is **data portability and trust**: imports are almost always one-way (Hevy only accepts Strong CSVs, and only once), AI workouts (Fitbod, Future) get repetitive after a few months, and coaching apps (Caliber, Future) routinely get accused of ghosting and overloaded coaches. An app that nails fast logging, a bulletproof watch app, real CSV export, and an honest one-time-purchase option directly attacks the weaknesses of every major incumbent.

---

## Key Findings

1. **The "Strong app paywall controversy" is the cautionary tale of the category.** Strong used to be a one-time purchase of roughly $10; it added a subscription tier of $4.99/month or $29.99/year plus a lifetime tier — per Dr. Muscle's review (updated April 26, 2025): *"A month of Strong PRO costs $4.99, a year is $29.99, and lifetime access is $99.99."* SensAI's 2026 comparison confirms the same: *"Strong remains the most affordable premium option at $29.99/yr, now with a $99.99 lifetime purchase."* The wave of one-star App Store reviews from when the model changed still anchors the page. Verbatim from current App Store reviews: *"BE WARNED: this app used to cost around $10 lifetime, but then the devs added a couple features and slapped the ridiculous price tag of $139 on it. AND made it a subscription app for $40/y! WHY does a LOG APP need a subscription!?"* and *"I can't even begin to understand why the developer thinks it appropriate to require users to pay a monthly subscription for it… an insulting money-grab."* Strong's own Terms of Service quietly defines "Forever" as the lifetime of the service, not the user — which lifters routinely call out as deceptive.

2. **StrongLifts 5x5 made the same mistake more aggressively and lost the program-app crown to Boostcamp.** The app is now, per the Google Play listing, *"free to download, but requires a subscription to use. All new users get a 7-day free trial for the yearly plan. Monthly and quarterly plans are also available without a free trial."* The yearly plan is around $30 (confirmed by App Store user reviews); no lifetime option is currently listed. Plate calculator, custom workouts, warm-up sets, and analytics — all previously free — are now paywalled. Top Play Store review (verbatim): *"Edit: Now you have to pay monthly. Get 5x5 workout app instead."* Reddit threads on r/Fitness and r/weightroom routinely redirect beginners to Boostcamp's free 5x5 templates.

3. **Hevy is the current Reddit darling, but it has a clear paywall-creep problem.** Per SensAI's 2026 head-to-head: *"In 2026, Hevy Pro costs $2.99/month, $23.99/year, or $74.99 once for lifetime access"* — corroborated by PRPath's 2026 Hevy review citing it as *"the cheapest Pro pricing in the category."* Pro gates custom-exercise cap expansion, advanced charts, plate-calculator nuances, body-measurement charts, and routine-folder organization. App Store complaints mirror Strong's earlier trajectory: *"I want to buy the lifetime feature instead of just having a subscription… I'm just not sure if I want to spend on Hevy."* Users also report Apple Watch black-screen bugs and rest-timer haptics that lag behind the phone. Trainer-side users on Capterra complain they *"can't access programs I built on my personal page"* when toggling between Hevy and Hevy Coach — i.e., cross-product data is siloed.

4. **Fitbod's AI is the most over-promised feature in the category.** Fitbod costs $15.99/month or $95.99/year, with only a 3-workout free trial — enough to see the UI but not enough to evaluate the algorithm, which itself *"needs 10–15 workouts of input data before personalisation reaches its full quality"* (Indie Hackers, Fitbod review, 2026). Recurring Reddit complaints aggregated in Dr. Muscle's review include: *"insisting on hitting quads and hamstrings daily, proposing absurd amounts of bodyweight exercises, and glitches that lead to duplicate or missing exercise records."* Long-term Trustpilot users rate it 4–5 stars; short-term users hammer it for subscription billing issues (Apple-side refunds the developer can't grant), out-of-sync Apple Watch (timer randomly stops on the watch but works on the phone), poor superset pairing that wastes equipment time, and an algorithm that's "good enough but not optimised" for periodized strength work.

5. **Jefit is the "outdated UI + ads" cautionary tale.** Free tier is monetized with banner/interstitial ads; exercise videos are paywalled to Elite. Reddit verdict (compiled by Setgraph and Dr. Muscle): rich exercise database (1,300+ exercises is also a curse — overwhelming and cluttered), and *"a recent update seems to have lowered the quality of the free version."* Many lifters stay on Jefit only because they have years of history they can't easily export.

6. **Gymshark Training is free and ad-free, but Reddit and App Store complaints are brutal on bugs and data loss.** Dr. Muscle's review of user feedback documents two repeated complaints: *"Gymshark offers a large collection of short-term workout programs… they cycle others out… it sucks when you're in the middle of a program you like and it's suddenly erased"* and *"Another technical issue has to do with the app erasing people's data… this seems to happen extensively after updates."* The Android app was discontinued (per Gymshark's own support page: *"the app is no longer available to download on Android devices"*), which is a near-fatal complaint pattern for an Android-targeted launch. Other recurring App Store gripes: no Apple Watch app, no favorite/save-for-later workouts, can't add supersets/drop sets, no real progress tracking, and "missing some pretty basic exercises."

7. **FitNotes is the gold standard for what users actually want — and it's free, Android-only, and barely marketed.** Reddit consensus (r/Fitness, r/weightroom): fast logging, no ads, no sync drama, no AI. The fact that the most-loved tracker on Reddit has zero monetization is the strongest signal in the category: **the bar for "this is worth paying for" is enormously high because the free baseline is already excellent.** Setgraph's Reddit-summary explicitly flags: *"FitNotes is excellent, but being Android-only is a dealbreaker for some users"* — a clear opening for an iOS+Android app that matches FitNotes's speed.

8. **Caliber's coaching model fails on two fronts: paywall-before-evaluation and coach ghosting.** Representative JustUseApp reviews: *"This app is a scam. The big problem is that you are not allowed to consult with one of their so-called 'coaches' without first plunking down $600 for a three-month commitment"*; *"I fell behind on logging workouts for a week and noticed the trainer did not mention anything… After about 2.5 weeks of not noticing whether or not I was actually working out, I decided to end the subscription"*; *"The whole 2 months I was active in the subscription, the workout never changed."*

9. **Future ($199/month) is collapsing under its own coaching economics.** Future's own App Store listing confirms: *"Future Pro membership is $199/month."* Trustpilot review (employee-adjacent): *"Coaches are forced to take on 85 clients! with poor compensation… In a short period of time I was transferred around several times when coaches kept disappearing without notice."* User-side: *"The workouts started to get repetitive, like there was a basic workout template that they were just tweaking… I'm paying $150 a month for trainers who don't have the time."* Plus auto-renewal complaints and Apple Watch sync issues. The "human coach in your pocket" promise rarely survives unit economics.

10. **Centr (Chris Hemsworth) gets accused of refund refusals and dark-pattern auto-renewals.** Representative user complaints: *"I started a free trial… and before my trial was up, I was charged $119 for a 1-year subscription. Upon asking customer service to cancel and refund me, they told me no"*; *"I had a 12-month free trial and I realised it was not what I wanted after one day. Cancelled it… however one year later I was billed with $120. They refuse to refund"*; *"They basically don't send communications after the first few days of joining."* Whatever else Centr does, this is a customer-trust dumpster fire and a template to avoid in every detail.

11. **MyFitnessPal lit its own brand on fire by paywalling the barcode scanner.** MyFitnessPal announced the change on August 24, 2022, with the paywall taking effect October 1, 2022, per Droid-Life: *"MyFitnessPal… announced this morning to its users that it is placing its fantastic barcode scanner feature behind a paywall beginning October 1."* Premium was $19.99/month or $79.99/year at the time (Pocket-lint), and the change triggered a mass migration to Cronometer (whose free tier still includes a scanner). The lesson is universal: **never paywall a feature users perceive as core/free.** It's the single fastest way to turn loyal users into evangelists for a competitor.

12. **The migration problem is industry-wide — and it's a moat for whoever solves it.** Hevy support is explicit: *"At this time, we only allow importing from the Strong app"*, and *"You can only do one data import. If you have performed a data import and wish to remove it, you can revert and remove the imported data."* Hevy also tells users converting from other apps: *"our team unfortunately doesn't have the manpower to assist with reformatting"* — i.e., the burden is on the user. No major app provides clean two-way CSV that survives round-trips. This is a feature you can ship in week one and brand around.

13. **Apple Watch / Wear OS is where every app is weakest.** Reported pattern across Fitbod, Hevy, Future, Strong: rest-timer drift between watch and phone; black-screen crashes; loss of sets when the watch loses connection mid-workout; over-reliance on the phone for any non-trivial action. Wear OS support across the board is even thinner — Strong has Apple Watch support but no Wear OS equivalent; Hevy and Fitbod have only recently caught up; Gymshark, Caliber, and Centr have nothing meaningful.

14. **AI fitness assistants are the most over-claimed feature in 2025.** Beyond Fitbod, every other AI-driven app (Future, JuggernautAI, Dr. Muscle, Caliber's smart programming) gets the same criticism on Reddit: programs become repetitive after 2–3 months, weight suggestions are either too aggressive (injury risk for beginners) or too conservative (boring for advanced lifters), and "smart substitutions" don't account for the gym you're actually in.

## App-by-App Breakdown (Major Recurring Complaints)

### Strong (Strong Workout Tracker)
- **Pricing/subscription:** The defining grievance. Originally ~$10 one-time → added $4.99/month, $29.99/year, plus a $99.99 lifetime tier. "Forever" defined in ToS as the lifetime of the *service*, not the user.
- **UI/UX:** Reviewers call the interface *"a trip back to 2015"* (Dr. Muscle); functional but no programming/coaching layer; you log sets, but the app assumes you already know what to log.
- **Features missing:** No built-in programs, weak superset/drop-set handling, no RPE field by default, no body-measurement charting depth.
- **Sentiment:** Established lifters still tolerate it; new users churning to Hevy.

### Hevy
- **Strengths Reddit loves:** Clean UI, fast logging, optional social feed, free tier is genuinely usable.
- **Complaints:** Pro paywall creeping (custom-exercise cap, plate-calculator nuances, advanced charts, routine folders, body-measurement charts). Apple Watch black-screen bugs reported on the App Store. Trainer-product (Hevy Coach) data siloed from personal account. Lifetime price point ($74.99) creates buyer's hesitation.
- **One-way import:** Only from Strong, only once.

### Jefit
- **UI:** Outdated and cluttered; 1,300+ exercises but poor navigation.
- **Ads:** Free tier is heavy with interstitials; exercise videos paywalled.
- **Updates:** A recent update *"lowered the quality of the free version"* (Dr. Muscle compiling user reviews).
- **Lock-in:** Many users feel trapped by years of history.

### Gymshark Training
- **Free and ad-free** — strong positives — but:
- **Data loss after updates** is a documented, repeated complaint.
- **Programs disappear mid-cycle** as Gymshark rotates content.
- **No Android app** (officially discontinued).
- **No Apple Watch app**, no favorites, no supersets/drop sets, exercise library has obvious gaps.
- **Brand-side problem:** Trustpilot complaints about Gymshark customer service (chatbot loops, partial refunds) bleed over.

### Fitbod
- **Pricing:** $15.99/month or $95.99/year; 3-workout trial too short to evaluate the AI.
- **AI:** Repetitive prescriptions, illogical muscle-group cycling, awkward superset pairing on shared equipment, miscalibrated weight suggestions.
- **Apple Watch sync:** Timer drift between watch and phone is the single most-cited bug.
- **Support:** Apple-purchased subscriptions can't be refunded by Fitbod directly — frequent angry reviews about being bounced between Apple and Fitbod.
- **For advanced lifters:** "Good enough but not optimised" for periodization, peaking, Sheiko/Smolov/Westside.

### Caliber
- **$600 / 3-month minimum** before you can even talk to a coach — top complaint.
- **Coach ghosting** is the second top complaint (2.5 weeks of zero contact).
- **Static workouts:** Some users report the same plan for 2 months.
- **Credentials opacity:** Users can't verify trainers are actually certified.

### Boostcamp
- **Best-loved free tier** in the program-following category. Per Boostcamp's own features page: *"130+ coach-designed programs from Alberto Nuñez, Eric Helms, Geoffrey Schofield, Cody Lefever, Jim Wendler, and others, plus 11,000+ community programs covering every level and goal. All free."* The App Store listing adds: *"Trusted by over 1,000,000 lifters. 300M+ workouts logged."* Boostcamp Premium is $60/year (per Setgraph's 2024 comparison) — far cheaper than Fitbod.
- **Complaints are mild:** Some advanced analytics gated behind Pro; some users want more granular customization within prescribed programs.
- **Strategic implication:** Boostcamp is winning by being honest about what's free vs. paid — copy this transparency.

### StrongLifts 5x5
- **Subscription transition torpedoed reviews.** Top Play Store complaint: *"Now you have to pay monthly. Get 5x5 workout app instead."*
- **Features paywalled** that used to be free: plate calculator, custom workouts, warm-up sets, analytics.
- **Strategic implication:** Users still want StrongLifts the *program* — they just refuse to pay for it. Boostcamp now hosts it free.

### Stronger by the Day (Bret Contreras / Bryce Lewis-style program-distribution apps)
- Limited Reddit coverage retrievable; main complaint pattern across this category of "coach-authored program apps" is **rigidity** (you can't customize the prescribed program) and **subscription stacking** (paying for the program *and* the app).

### Liftin / Setgraph / Strong Workout Tracker (small indie trackers)
- **Setgraph** is positioned as a Reddit-friendly minimalist alternative (note that Setgraph also publishes its own "what Reddit thinks" SEO content, so treat its self-summaries as marketing).
- **Liftin** is mentioned only in passing on Reddit; coverage is too thin to draw a pattern. Indie trackers generally win on simplicity but lose on cross-platform sync and watch apps.

### Nike Training Club
- **Free tier has shrunk** over time; was briefly all-free during 2020 and rolled back. Reddit gripes: limited strength tracking (it's primarily a guided-workout streaming app, not a logger); poor Apple Watch integration; programs feel marketing-driven.

### MyFitnessPal
- **2022 barcode-scanner paywall** is the watershed event — announced August 24, 2022, effective October 1, 2022. Mass migration to Cronometer.
- **Other complaints:** food database polluted with user-submitted garbage entries; ads on free tier; constant upsell modals.

### Future
- **$199/month + 85 clients per coach** = repetitive workouts and ghosting (Trustpilot pattern).
- **Auto-renewal complaints** common.
- **Apple Watch sync issues** reported.

### Centr
- **Refund refusal** after auto-renewal of a trial — egregious, dark-pattern reviews dominate Trustpilot and JustUseApp.
- **Content quality** described as basic for $30/month.

### Freeletics
- **Free trial → forced subscription** complaints; AI claims that don't materialize for strength training; user community on Reddit largely shifted to other apps for lifting (Freeletics is bodyweight-strong but not a barbell tracker).

## Pattern Analysis: Mistakes Multiple Apps Make

1. **Paywalling features that used to be free.** Strong (lifters' uproar 2018–19), StrongLifts (full subscription wall), MyFitnessPal (Oct 2022 barcode scanner). The Reddit memory is long, and the App Store one-star reviews persist for years.
2. **"Lifetime" that isn't.** Strong's ToS, Future's coach turnover, and the broader pattern documented in DEV Community's "Lifetime subscriptions don't mean what you think they mean" piece (including Pocket Casts, SiriusXM, and AppSumo case studies).
3. **Apple Watch as an afterthought.** Across Fitbod, Hevy, Future, Strong: timer drift, sync drops, black screens. Wear OS is even worse.
4. **One-way imports / no exports.** Hevy only ingests Strong CSV, once. Most apps lock data in proprietary schemas.
5. **AI that flattens out after 60–90 days.** Fitbod, Future. Users notice the template-tweaking pattern fast.
6. **Customer support that points the finger at Apple/Google.** Fitbod, Centr — when an App Store subscription goes wrong, support disclaims responsibility.
7. **Free trials with dark-pattern auto-renew.** Centr is the worst offender; Future, Fitbod, Freeletics all draw similar (if less extreme) complaints.
8. **Updates that delete data.** Gymshark Training is the worst documented case; Hevy and Fitbod have isolated reports.
9. **Coaching apps overloading coaches.** Future's *"85 clients per coach"* is the clearest example, but Caliber shows the same dynamic at a lower price.
10. **Cluttered/old UIs that assume desktop-style information density.** Jefit, Strong, Future. Gym-floor usability (gloves, sweat, one-handed) almost never tested.
11. **Missing fundamentals:** Many apps still lack proper supersets, drop sets, AMRAP, RPE, percentage-based programming, deload weeks, machine-specific tracking, or a plate calculator outside of a paywall.

## Features Users Repeatedly Say They Want (And Can't Find)

- **A 2-tap set-logger that shows last week's numbers on the same screen** — universally praised on Reddit; almost no app does it with zero friction.
- **A reliable Apple Watch app that works standalone**, including a rest timer with haptics that never desyncs from the phone.
- **A real plate calculator** not behind a paywall.
- **First-class RPE + percentage-based programming** for strength programs (Sheiko/Smolov/5/3/1/Conjugate).
- **Drop sets, supersets, AMRAP, myo-reps, cluster sets, rest-pause** — all as first-class citizens, not workarounds.
- **Two-way CSV/JSON export** that round-trips losslessly.
- **Photo progress and body-measurement tracking** integrated with the lifting data (most apps have one but not the other).
- **An "import from anywhere" wizard** that accepts Strong, Hevy, Jefit, FitNotes, StrongLifts, and generic CSV.
- **Apple Health / Google Fit / Garmin / Strava** two-way integration.
- **Calendar view with history search** (most apps only let you scroll back).
- **Offline-first** logging that never loses a set when sync fails.
- **Honest one-time-purchase tier** for users who refuse subscriptions.

## Pricing / Monetization Lessons

- **What works on Reddit:**
  - Generous free tier that's actually usable forever (Boostcamp, FitNotes, Hevy v1).
  - $3–$5/month or $25–$40/year if Pro is truly differentiated (Hevy's $2.99/$23.99 is the cheapest reference point in the category).
  - Lifetime tier priced honestly (~3–4× annual; Hevy $74.99 and Strong $99.99 are the market benchmarks) and *honored honestly.*
  - Free trial with no credit card OR with a pre-charge-day reminder email.
- **What detonates trust:**
  - Moving previously free features behind a paywall (Strong, StrongLifts, MyFitnessPal).
  - "Lifetime" defined as the lifetime of the service, not the user.
  - 3-workout free "trial" too short to evaluate (Fitbod).
  - Auto-renew with no email reminder (Centr).
  - Refund refusals routed through Apple (Fitbod, Centr).
  - Mid-program content removal on free tiers (Gymshark).
  - Paywalled barcode scanner / plate calculator / basic features (MyFitnessPal, StrongLifts).

**Recommended model for your app:** Free tier is unlimited workouts + unlimited routines + plate calculator + CSV export + Apple Health sync (i.e., everything most apps charge for). Pro tier ($3.99/month, $29.99/year, $89 lifetime — priced just under Strong, just above Hevy) adds: advanced analytics, AI form/program suggestions, cloud backup beyond X months, photo progress, custom theming. Crucially: **commit publicly to never moving a free feature behind the paywall**, and put that commitment in your app description and FAQ. That single promise is a differentiator on Reddit.

## Trust & Data Portability

- Build a "Your Data" screen with: full CSV export (sets, reps, weight, RPE, notes, dates), JSON export, one-click account deletion with a clear timeline, and a printable PDF history.
- Accept imports from Strong, Hevy, Jefit, FitNotes, StrongLifts (and generic CSV with a column-mapping wizard).
- Publish a "what happens if we shut down" promise: data dump emailed to every user, format/schema published.
- GDPR/CCPA-compliant data download in under a minute, no support ticket required.
- Don't sell data, don't run third-party ad SDKs in the free tier (banner ads are an instant 1-star trigger on Reddit — see Jefit).

## Apple Watch / Wear OS Implementation Lessons

- **Standalone-first.** The watch app must log a full workout with the phone off or absent. Strong and Hevy partially do this; Fitbod doesn't reliably.
- **Single source of truth for the rest timer.** It should never drift between watch and phone. The timer is the single most-complained-about feature across Fitbod's reviews: *"On my watch the timer randomly stops while on the phone it works fine."*
- **Haptic-only rest cues** with strong patterns (gloves + sweat + loud gym = no eyes on screen).
- **Crown for weight increments**, large tap targets for reps.
- **Wear OS parity from launch.** Almost no incumbent has serious Wear OS support — this is a near-empty market segment.
- **Auto-sync on reconnect**, with conflict resolution that defaults to "keep both" rather than "overwrite."
- **No login walls** on the watch.

## Recommendations (Staged, with Trigger Thresholds)

### Stage 1 — Pre-launch (next 60 days)
1. **Build the 2-tap logger.** If your time-to-log a repeat set isn't under 3 seconds, fix it before anything else. Benchmark: log 10 sets in under 30 seconds, one-handed, with gym gloves.
2. **Ship a bulletproof rest timer first.** Offline, on watch and phone, with haptics, zero drift. This is your #1 differentiator on day one.
3. **Ship import-from-everywhere.** Strong, Hevy, FitNotes, StrongLifts, Jefit, generic CSV. Two-way export from day one.
4. **Free-tier promise.** Commit publicly to: unlimited workouts/routines, plate calculator, CSV export, Apple Health sync — *forever free, forever in writing.*

### Stage 2 — Launch (months 1–3)
1. **Apple Watch + Wear OS parity at launch.** Standalone logging, haptics, crown for weight. Skip this and you're in the same complaint bucket as Fitbod and Hevy.
2. **Supersets, drop sets, AMRAP, RPE, %1RM as first-class objects**, not bolt-ons.
3. **Pricing: $3.99/month, $29.99/year, $89 lifetime — with a 14-day no-CC-needed trial of Pro.** Email reminder 3 days before any charge.
4. **Trust signals on the App Store page**: "We will never paywall a feature that's free today" + "Full data export anytime" + "Delete your account in one tap."

### Stage 3 — Months 3–12
1. **Add programs (Boostcamp-style library) and a program-builder** with proper progression, deload weeks, periodization. License or commission well-known programs (5/3/1, GZCLP, nSuns, PPL).
2. **AI cautiously.** Don't lead with it. If you add AI workout generation, frame it as a *suggestion engine* over user-defined templates, not a black box. Avoid Fitbod's "repetitive after 90 days" trap by training the AI on RPE + bar-speed inputs (or velocity-tracking integrations) rather than just sets/reps.
3. **Coaching marketplace (optional).** If you go here, **cap clients per coach at 25**, publish coach credentials, and never paywall the first consultation. Caliber and Future have shown the bear traps.

### Triggers to revisit pricing/strategy
- If churn after free trial exceeds **35%**: trial is too short or Pro features aren't compelling. Likely the latter.
- If App Store reviews trend below 4.5 stars: search 1-star reviews for the words "subscription," "paywall," "Apple Watch," "lost data," "timer." Treat any of those terms appearing in >10% of negative reviews as a P0.
- If a competitor (Strong, Hevy) changes its pricing model: run a migration campaign within 48 hours with a one-click import flow.

## Caveats

- **Source quality:** The web_search budget was exhausted before I could pull a large volume of primary Reddit threads directly, and the Reddit URL-fetch endpoint was restricted in this environment. The verbatim quotes above are drawn from App Store, Google Play, Trustpilot, JustUseApp, Capterra, and Product Hunt reviews, plus review-site compilations (Dr. Muscle, Setgraph, Indie Hackers, Tom's Guide, SensAI, PRPath, Droid-Life, Pocket-lint) that themselves aggregate Reddit sentiment. The *patterns* described — paywall backlash, Apple Watch sync, AI repetition, coach ghosting — are well-documented across these sources and consistent with Reddit discussions, but specific Reddit thread URLs and upvote counts are not cited here.
- **Apps with thin coverage:** Stronger by the Day, Liftin/Setgraph (as a competitor app), Freeletics, Nike Training Club. Recommendations about these are inferred from category patterns, not direct evidence.
- **Recency:** Strong's paywall controversy peaked in 2018–19; Hevy's free tier was more generous through 2023. Some complaints may be partially addressed in current versions — verify current state at launch time.
- **Setgraph as a source:** Setgraph publishes "what Reddit recommends" SEO content but is also a competitor app — treat its summaries as directional, not neutral.
- **Forward-looking claims** (e.g., "an Apple Watch–first app would win") are inferences based on the absence of a strong incumbent, not predictions.
- **Pricing volatility:** All subscription prices were accurate as of early-to-mid 2026 sources; app pricing in this category changes frequently. Re-verify before any competitive positioning is finalized.
